# Teacher evaluation protocol

This review is meant to test whether TeachKit produces material a teacher could use after a quick check. It is not a classroom study, and it does not measure student learning outcomes.

## Participant instructions

Ask each participant to review the same original TeachKit water-cycle sample. Use the live GPT-5.6 deployment only after it passes validation. Do not use student names, handwriting, school records, or copyrighted textbook pages.

Tell participants:

> TeachKit turns one source page into three worksheets, an exit quiz, discussion prompts, and a parent note. Please review the generated pack as if you were preparing tomorrow's class. The review should take about ten minutes.

## Questions

Record role, teaching experience range, subject, and grade range. Do not collect names unless the participant explicitly agrees.

Rate each statement from 1 to 5, where 1 means strongly disagree and 5 means strongly agree:

1. The lesson objective matches the source.
2. The facts and vocabulary are correct.
3. Guided Practice provides useful support without giving away every answer.
4. Independent Practice requires students to apply the idea themselves.
5. Challenge requires deeper reasoning than the other worksheets.
6. The exit quiz checks the stated objective.
7. The pages are readable and ready to print.
8. I would use this pack after reviewing it.

Ask three short questions:

1. How many minutes would this save compared with preparing the same material manually?
2. What is the first thing you would change?
3. Did any statement feel incorrect, unsafe, or inappropriate for the grade?

## Reporting rules

- Report the participant count and median rating for each category.
- Describe the sample as small and directional.
- Quote feedback only with permission and remove identifying details.
- Record at least one product change made because of repeated feedback.
- Do not claim improved learning outcomes without a separate classroom study.
